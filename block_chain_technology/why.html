Why Blockchain?

        The entire world runs on information, and the introduction of databases helped with storing the data. However, the lack of security features in a database is one of the challenges to its usage on the world wide web. One key difference between a typical database and a blockchain is how the data is structured. A blockchain collects information together in groups, known as blocks, that hold sets of information. Blocks have storage capacities and, when filled, are closed and linked to the previously filled block, forming a chain of data known as the blockchain. All new information that follows that freshly added block is compiled into a newly formed block that will then also be added to the chain once filled
